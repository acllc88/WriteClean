Free Grammar Checker with No Sign-Up: 7 Tools Tested in 2026

Quick Guide March 10, 2026 6 min read By WriteClean

If you've ever searched "free grammar checker no sign up" and immediately got asked for your email, you're not alone. We tested 7 popular options in 2026 to find which truly work without an account.

Quick answer: WriteClean, GrammarCheck.net, and LanguageTool's web version all work without an account. Most others require sign-up or impose word limits that force upgrades.

Why "No Sign-Up" Matters More Than Ever

The #1 Grammarly complaint on Trustpilot is billing — once your email is in a system, billing follows. A grammar checker that works without an account is faster, more private, and can't auto-charge your card. This became a major concern in 2025–2026 as reports of unexpected $144 annual charges spread across consumer complaint sites.

The 7 Tools We Tested

1. WriteClean (This Site)

The only tool we found in 2026 that is genuinely unlimited, free, and no-account with no auto-renewal. Voice-preserving mode means corrections don't come out sounding like a corporate press release. The AI humanizer tab is a unique bonus not found in other free tools. Best overall for no-sign-up grammar checking.

2. GrammarCheck.net

A simple browser-based editor that works without login. Limited to basic grammar and spelling — no AI rewrites, no style suggestions. Good for a quick check on shorter texts, but you'll want something more powerful for longer documents.

3. QuillBot Grammar Check

Technically no sign-up required for basic grammar, but the paraphrasing and humanizing features have restrictive word limits on the free tier. For grammar-only checks it functions, but you'll hit a paywall quickly on longer documents.

4. LanguageTool (Web Version)

The web version lets you check text without an account up to around 20,000 characters. Excellent for multilingual writers — supports 30+ languages. The free tier is more generous than most competitors. Recommended for non-English writers.

5. Grammarly

Requires account creation and email verification. The billing patterns have generated significant user complaints. Not recommended if you want the no-sign-up experience, and the auto-renewal risk is real.

6. Hemingway Editor (Web)

Works directly in the browser with no sign-up. Focuses on readability scoring rather than grammar corrections specifically. An excellent complement to a dedicated grammar checker but not a replacement.

7. ZeroGPT Grammar

No sign-up required for basic grammar checks. Clean interface and reasonably fast. Fewer features than WriteClean but functional for simple, short corrections.
