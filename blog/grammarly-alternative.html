Grammarly vs Free Alternatives: The Honest 2026 Breakdown

Comparison March 5, 2026 8 min read By WriteClean

Grammarly is the most well-known grammar tool on the planet. It also has a 1.7/5 rating on Trustpilot's complaint-focused review aggregators, with thousands of users reporting unauthorized charges, cancelled accounts that still get billed, and writing suggestions that strip all personality from their text. So: is it worth $144 a year?

Key finding: The core grammar features of Grammarly's free tier are available — better, in some cases — from tools like WriteClean that require no account, no billing, and no word limits.

What Grammarly's Trustpilot Reviews Actually Say

We analyzed Grammarly's reviews across Trustpilot, the Better Business Bureau, and consumer complaint sites. The complaints cluster into three categories consistently:

  • Billing issues — Users report being charged for annual plans they never consciously selected, with charges of €144–$175 appearing without clear warning after a free trial.
  • Robotic suggestions — Multiple reviewers describe their writing as losing its "voice" after accepting Grammarly's corrections. Professional emails start sounding like legal disclaimers.
  • Technical failures — The desktop app and browser extension frequently crash, causing writers to lose work in progress. This is the #3 complaint category by volume.

When Grammarly Is Worth It

Grammarly Premium genuinely excels at one thing: real-time correction across every app via its browser extension and desktop app. If you write in dozens of different tools and need corrections to follow you everywhere, the integration is unmatched in 2026. For pure quality of corrections — not integrated convenience — free alternatives are fully competitive.
