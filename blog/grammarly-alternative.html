What 2,000+ Grammarly Trustpilot Reviews Actually Say

The complaints cluster into three categories consistently. First, billing issues — users report being charged for annual plans they never consciously selected, with charges of €144–$175 appearing without clear warning after a free trial ends. Second, robotic suggestions — multiple reviewers describe their writing losing its voice after accepting Grammarly's corrections. Third, technical failures — the desktop app and browser extension frequently crash.

When Grammarly Premium Is Actually Worth It

If you write across dozens of apps — Gmail, Google Docs, Slack, Word, Notion — and need corrections to follow you everywhere automatically, Grammarly Premium's integration is genuinely unmatched. The browser extension catching errors in real-time as you type is something no free tool replicates. If that convenience is worth $144/year to you, Grammarly delivers it. Just set a calendar reminder before the trial ends.

Our verdict

For most people — students, professionals, ESL writers, freelancers — WriteClean covers 90% of what Grammarly Premium does, for free, with no account and no billing risk. For writers who need real-time correction across every app they use, Grammarly Premium is still the best in class. For paraphrasing specifically, QuillBot is the strongest free option. For long-form book or thesis writing, ProWritingAid's lifetime licence is the best long-term value.
